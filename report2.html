INTERNAL BULLETIN — PARENTAL REPORTS

CONFIDENTIAL • DEPARTMENT OF COMMUNITY SAFETY

FOLLOWING INITIAL PUBLIC MENTIONS, THIS OFFICE HAS RECEIVED MULTIPLE REPORTS FROM PARENTS AND GUARDIANS REGARDING A CONCENTRATED PATTERN OF CONSUMPTIVE BEHAVIOR AMONG MINORS ASSOCIATED WITH THE AFOREMENTIONED WEB MAZE. REPORTS INDICATE AN ESCALATING ATTACHMENT TO THE PLATFORM, INCLUDING FREQUENT NIGHT-TIME USAGE AND SOCIAL ISOLATION.

SEVERAL CASES DETAIL CHILDREN WHO INITIALLY PARTICIPATED IN GROUP SOLVING BUT LATER REDUCED COMMUNICATION WITH FAMILY MEMBERS, SPENT SIGNIFICANT TIME ATTEMPTING TO 'COMPLETE' THE MAZE, AND IN SOME INSTANCES WERE NOT LOCATED IN THEIR HOMES WHEN PARENTS RETURNED.

WE STRONGLY ADVISE CAREGIVERS TO REVIEW BROWSING HISTORIES, ESTABLISH SCREEN-TIME BOUNDARIES, AND MONITOR SOCIAL CHANNELS WHERE STRATEGIES AND SPOILERS ARE EXCHANGED. THIS OFFICE CONTINUES TO COORDINATE WITH LOCAL SCHOOLS AND CYBER SAFETY GROUPS TO PROVIDE RESOURCES.
