Labyrinth

Labyrinth refers to a complex and confusing series of pathways or passages, often used synonymously with a maze. Historically, the labyrinth is symbolic of a journey or pilgrimage, leading to personal transformation.

The most well-known labyrinth is the mythological structure designed by Daedalus in Crete to contain the Minotaur. Over time, labyrinths have appeared in numerous cultures and media, often representing a puzzle, challenge, or spiritual journey.

Unlike mazes, traditional labyrinths typically have only one path leading to the entrance and then to the center, without branches or dead ends. Mazes, on the other hand, include multiple paths, choices, and often misleading passages.

Modern Usage

Labyrinths are still constructed today for therapeutic, recreational, or ceremonial purposes. In literature and games, the labyrinth is frequently a symbol of entrapment or introspection.
